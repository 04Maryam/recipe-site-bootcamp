Biryani Recipe

Fragrant basmati rice cooked with spices, saffron, and your choice of meat or vegetables.

Ingredients

For the Rice:
  • 2 cups basmati rice, rinsed and soaked for 30 minutes
  • 4 cups water
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 4-5 cloves
  • 4-5 green cardamom pods
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
For the Marinade:
  • 1 pound (450g) chicken (bone-in or boneless) or vegetables (like potatoes, carrots, and beans)
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • Salt to taste
  • Juice of 1 lemon
For the Biryani Masala:
  • 3 tablespoons oil or ghee
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 medium tomatoes, chopped
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1/4 cup fresh mint leaves, chopped
Additional Ingredients:
  • 1/2 cup fried onions (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1/4 cup milk with a pinch of saffron strands soaked in it
  • 2 tablespoons melted butter or ghee

Preparation Time

  • Prep time: 30 minutes (plus 1 hour for marination)
  • Cook time: 45 minutes
  • Total time: ~1 hour 15 minutes

Instructions

  1. Marinate the Chicken or Vegetables:
    • Combine the chicken or vegetables with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, turmeric, red chili powder, garam masala, coriander, lemon juice, and salt.
    • Mix well, cover, and refrigerate for at least 1 hour.
  2. Prepare the Rice:
    • Bring 4 cups of water to a boil in a large pot. Add the bay leaf, cloves, cardamom, cinnamon, and salt.
    • Add the soaked rice and cook until it is 70-80% cooked (still slightly firm). Drain and set aside.
  3. Cook the Biryani Masala:
    • Heat oil or ghee in a large pan. Add cumin seeds and let them sizzle.
    • Add sliced onions and saut√© until golden brown.
    • Stir in the green chilies, tomatoes, ground cumin, coriander, turmeric, and garam masala. Cook until the tomatoes break down and the mixture thickens.
    • Add the marinated chicken or vegetables and cook until they are almost done (5-10 minutes for chicken, 10-15 minutes for vegetables).
  4. Layer the Biryani:
    • In a heavy-bottomed pot, layer half the cooked rice. Spread half the biryani masala and sprinkle some chopped cilantro, mint, and fried onions.
    • Add the remaining rice, then layer the remaining masala, cilantro, mint, and fried onions.
    • Drizzle the saffron-infused milk and melted butter or ghee on top.
  5. Dum Cooking (Sealing and Steaming):
    • Cover the pot tightly with a lid or aluminum foil to trap steam.
    • Cook on low heat for 20-25 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
  6. Serve:
    • Gently fluff the biryani with a fork. Serve hot with raita, salad, or pickle.

Enjoy the rich and aromatic Biryani, a timeless dish cherished across many regions!
