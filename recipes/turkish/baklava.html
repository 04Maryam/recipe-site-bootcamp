Baklava Recipe

Sweet pastry with layers of phyllo dough, nuts, and syrup.

Ingredients

For the Baklava:
  • 1 lb (450g) phyllo dough (about 20 sheets)
  • 2 cups mixed nuts (typically pistachios, walnuts, and hazelnuts), finely chopped
  • 1 1/2 cups unsalted butter, melted
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon (optional)
For the Syrup:
  • 2 cups granulated sugar
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 cinnamon stick (optional)
  • 1-2 strips of lemon peel (optional)

Preparation Time

  • Prep time: 30 minutes
  • Cook time: 45-50 minutes
  • Total time: ~1.5 hours

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Syrup:
    • In a small saucepan, combine the sugar, water, lemon juice, honey, cinnamon stick, and lemon peel (if using).
    • Bring to a boil over medium heat, stirring occasionally.
    • Once it starts to boil, reduce the heat and let it simmer for about 10-15 minutes, until the syrup thickens slightly.
    • Remove from heat and let the syrup cool while you prepare the baklava.
  2. Prepare the Baklava:
    • Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
    • Brush the bottom of a 9x13-inch baking dish with some of the melted butter.
    • Place the first sheet of phyllo dough in the dish and brush it lightly with more melted butter.
    • Continue layering and buttering phyllo sheets, one at a time, until you have about 8-10 sheets layered at the bottom of the dish.
    • After the base layer is ready, spread a thin layer of the chopped nuts evenly over the phyllo dough. You can also add a pinch of ground cinnamon to the nuts if you like.
    • Continue layering 2-3 sheets of phyllo dough, followed by another layer of nuts. Repeat this process until all the nuts are used up, and finish with about 8-10 more layers of phyllo dough on top.
  3. Cut the Baklava:
    • Before baking, use a sharp knife to cut the baklava into diamond or square shapes. This makes it easier to serve after baking.
  4. Bake the Baklava:
    • Bake the baklava in the preheated oven for 45-50 minutes, or until the phyllo dough is golden and crispy.
    • Keep an eye on it toward the end to ensure it doesn't burn, as oven temperatures may vary.
  5. Pour the Syrup:
    • Once the baklava is baked, remove it from the oven and immediately pour the cooled syrup evenly over the hot baklava.
    • Be sure to cover the entire surface with syrup. The baklava will soak up the syrup as it cools.
    • Let the baklava sit at room temperature for at least 4 hours, or ideally overnight, to allow the syrup to soak through and the layers to become beautifully sticky.
  6. Serve:
    • Once the baklava has fully absorbed the syrup, it is ready to be served.
    • Garnish with extra chopped pistachios on top (optional).
    • Serve at room temperature or slightly chilled.

Baklava is a rich, sweet pastry made from layers of buttery phyllo dough, chopped nuts, and a syrup made of sugar, honey, and lemon. The result is a dessert with a delightful crunch and a satisfying sweetness that's perfect for special occasions or a treat with a cup of Turkish tea.
