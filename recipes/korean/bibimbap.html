Bibimbap Recipe

Rice topped with sautéed vegetables, meat, and a fried egg, served with gochujang (chili paste).

Ingredients

For the Rice:
  • 2 cups (400g) short-grain white rice (or sushi rice)
  • 4 cups (960ml) water
For the Toppings:
  • 1/2 lb (225g) ground beef or thinly sliced beef (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil (for beef)
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce (for beef)
  • 1 teaspoon garlic, minced (for beef)
  • 2 large eggs (for frying)
  • 1/2 cup spinach, blanched
  • 1 medium carrot, julienned
  • 1 zucchini, julienned
  • 1/2 cup shiitake or button mushrooms, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup bean sprouts, blanched
  • 2-3 tablespoons gochujang (Korean red chili paste)
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil (for vegetables)
  • 1 teaspoon sesame seeds
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • Salt to taste

Preparation Time

  • Prep time: 20 minutes
  • Cook time: 30 minutes
  • Total time: ~50 minutes

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Rice:
    • Rinse the rice under cold water until the water runs clear.
    • Cook the rice according to the package instructions or in a rice cooker. Once cooked, fluff and set aside.
  2. Prepare the Beef (Optional):
    • In a small pan, heat 1 tablespoon of sesame oil over medium heat.
    • Add the ground beef or sliced beef and cook until browned.
    • Add soy sauce, garlic, and a pinch of salt, and cook for an additional 1-2 minutes until the beef is fully cooked. Set aside.
  3. Prepare the Vegetables:
    • Spinach: Blanch the spinach in boiling water for 1-2 minutes, then drain and squeeze out excess water. Toss with 1 teaspoon sesame oil, a pinch of salt, and sesame seeds.
    • Carrot: Lightly sauté the julienned carrots in a pan with a small amount of sesame oil for 2-3 minutes until just tender. Set aside.
    • Zucchini: Sauté the zucchini in a pan with sesame oil for 2-3 minutes until tender but still crisp. Set aside.
    • Mushrooms: Sauté the mushrooms in a little sesame oil for 2-3 minutes until soft. Set aside.
    • Bean Sprouts: Blanch the bean sprouts in boiling water for 1-2 minutes, then drain.
  4. Fry the Eggs:
    • In a separate pan, fry the eggs sunny-side up until the whites are set but the yolk is still runny.
  5. Assemble the Bibimbap:
    • Place a serving of rice in the center of each bowl.
    • Arrange the beef, spinach, carrots, zucchini, mushrooms, and bean sprouts in separate sections around the rice.
    • Top with a fried egg in the center.
  6. Serve with Gochujang:
    • Add a dollop of gochujang (Korean chili paste) on the side of each bowl for heat and flavor.
    • Drizzle a little sesame oil and sprinkle sesame seeds on top.
  7. Mix and Enjoy:
    • To eat, stir everything together, mixing the rice, vegetables, beef, egg, and gochujang into a deliciously flavorful combination.

Enjoy this vibrant and flavorful Korean classic, packed with various textures and rich umami!
