Kimchi Jigae Recipe

Spicy stew made with kimchi, tofu, and seafood.

Ingredients

For the Stew:
  • 1 1/2 cups (300g) well-fermented kimchi, chopped (preferably aged kimchi)
  • 1/2 lb (225g) pork belly or beef (optional), sliced thinly
  • 1 medium onion, sliced
  • 2-3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon gochugaru (Korean red pepper flakes) (optional for heat)
  • 1 tablespoon gochujang (Korean red chili paste)
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 4 cups (960ml) water or anchovy broth (for a richer flavor)
  • 1/2 block (200g) tofu, cut into cubes
  • 1 teaspoon soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sugar (optional, to balance the sourness of the kimchi)
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt (to taste)
  • 2 green onions, chopped (for garnish)
  • 1/2 teaspoon sesame seeds (optional for garnish)

Preparation Time

  • Prep time: 10 minutes
  • Cook time: 25-30 minutes
  • Total time: ~40 minutes

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Ingredients:
    • Chop the kimchi into bite-sized pieces. If you’re using pork belly or beef, slice it thinly.
    • Cut the tofu into cubes and set aside.
    • Slice the onion and mince the garlic.
  2. Cook the Meat (Optional):
    • In a large pot, heat 1 tablespoon of sesame oil over medium heat.
    • Add the sliced pork or beef and sauté until the meat is browned and cooked through. Remove the meat from the pot and set aside.
  3. Sauté Aromatics:
    • In the same pot, add the sliced onion and minced garlic. Sauté for 2-3 minutes until fragrant and softened.
  4. Add the Kimchi:
    • Add the chopped kimchi to the pot and sauté for 3-4 minutes, allowing the kimchi to cook down and release its flavors.
  5. Add Broth and Seasonings:
    • Add the water or anchovy broth to the pot and bring to a boil. If you prefer a richer flavor, anchovy broth is a great option, but water will work just fine.
    • Stir in the gochujang, gochugaru (if using), soy sauce, and sugar (if desired). Stir well to combine.
  6. Simmer the Stew:
    • Reduce the heat to medium-low and let the stew simmer for 15-20 minutes, allowing the kimchi to soften and infuse the broth with its flavor.
  7. Add the Tofu:
    • Gently add the tofu cubes to the pot and simmer for an additional 5-7 minutes, allowing the tofu to absorb the flavors of the stew.
  8. Season and Garnish:
    • Taste the stew and adjust seasoning with salt if needed.
    • Once done, sprinkle with chopped green onions and sesame seeds.
  9. Serve:
    • Serve hot with steamed rice and enjoy the warm, spicy, and comforting flavors of Kimchi Jjigae!

Kimchi Jjigae is a staple in Korean households, perfect for using up older kimchi as it becomes even more flavorful as it ages. Enjoy!
