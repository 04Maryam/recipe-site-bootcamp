Croissant Recipe

Flaky, buttery pastry perfect for breakfast.

Ingredients

For the Dough:
  • 4 cups (500g) all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup (50g) granulated sugar
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 1 tablespoon instant yeast
  • 1 cup (240ml) warm milk
  • 1/4 cup (60ml) warm water
  • 3 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted
For the Butter Layer:
  • 1 cup (225g) unsalted butter, cold but pliable
For Finishing:
  • 1 egg, beaten (for egg wash)

Preparation Time

  • Prep time: 1 hour
  • Resting/Chilling time: 6-8 hours (or overnight)
  • Cook time: 20-25 minutes
  • Total time: ~8-10 hours/li>

Instructions

  1. Make the Dough:
    • In a large bowl, mix the flour, sugar, salt, and yeast. Add the warm milk, warm water, and melted butter. Mix until a sticky dough forms.
    • Knead the dough for 8-10 minutes until smooth and elastic. Cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate for 1 hour.
  2. Prepare the Butter Layer:
    • Place the butter between two sheets of parchment paper. Use a rolling pin to flatten it into a 7x7-inch (18x18 cm) square. Chill the butter square in the fridge until firm but pliable.
  3. Laminate the Dough:
    • Roll the dough into a 10x10-inch (25x25 cm) square. Place the butter square diagonally in the center of the dough square. Fold the corners of the dough over the butter to completely encase it, like an envelope.
    • Roll the dough into a 20x10-inch (50x25 cm) rectangle. Fold it into thirds like a letter. Wrap in plastic and chill for 30 minutes.
    • Repeat this rolling and folding process 2 more times, chilling the dough for 30 minutes between each fold.
  4. Shape the Croissants:
    • Roll the laminated dough into a 20x12-inch (50x30 cm) rectangle. Cut the dough into long triangles (base of each triangle ~3 inches wide).
    • Roll each triangle from the base to the tip, forming the classic croissant shape. Place on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  5. Proof the Croissants:
    • Cover the shaped croissants with a damp cloth or plastic wrap. Let them rise in a warm place for 1.5-2 hours, until doubled in size.
  6. Bake:
    • Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Brush the croissants with beaten egg for a golden finish.
    • Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until golden brown and flaky.
  7. Serve:
    • Let the croissants cool slightly before serving. Enjoy them plain or with butter, jam, or chocolate.

Homemade croissants are a labor of love, but the buttery, flaky results are well worth the effort!
