Baklava Recipe

A rich pastry made of layers of phyllo dough, nuts and honey.

Ingredients

For the Baklava:
  • 1 package (16 oz/450g) phyllo dough, thawed
  • 2 cups (300g) mixed nuts (walnuts, pistachios, and almonds), finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cloves (optional)
  • 2 cups (400g) granulated sugar
  • 1 cup (240g) unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 tablespoon honey
For the Syrup:
  • 1 1/2 cups (375ml) water
  • 1 1/2 cups (300g) granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup (120ml) honey
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1-2 strips of lemon peel (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Preparation Time

  • Prep time: 30 minutes
  • Cook time: 40-45 minutes
  • Resting time: 2-3 hours (for cooling and soaking in syrup)
  • Total time: ~3 hours

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Nut Filling:
    • In a bowl, combine the chopped nuts, cinnamon, and cloves (if using). Set aside.
  2. Prepare the Phyllo Dough:
    • Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
    • Brush a 9x13-inch baking dish with melted butter.
    • Place a sheet of phyllo dough in the dish, then brush it lightly with more butter. Repeat this process, layering and buttering each sheet (you’ll use about 10 sheets for the bottom layer).
  3. Assemble the Baklava:
    • Once the base is prepared, sprinkle a thin layer of the nut mixture over the phyllo dough.
    • Add another 4-5 sheets of phyllo, buttering each layer.
    • Repeat layering the nuts and phyllo dough until all of the nuts are used, finishing with a final layer of 10-12 sheets of phyllo dough on top, buttering each layer.
  4. Cut the Baklava:
    • Using a sharp knife, carefully cut the baklava into diamond or square shapes, cutting through all the layers.
  5. Bake:
    • Bake the baklava in the preheated oven for 40-45 minutes, or until it's golden brown and crisp.
    • While the baklava is baking, prepare the syrup.
  6. Make the Syrup:
    • In a saucepan, combine the water, sugar, honey, cinnamon stick, and lemon peel (if using).
    • Bring to a boil, then lower the heat and simmer for about 10 minutes, until the syrup thickens slightly.
    • Remove from heat and stir in the vanilla extract. Allow to cool to room temperature.
  7. Soak the Baklava:
    • Once the baklava is out of the oven, immediately pour the cooled syrup evenly over the hot baklava. Be sure to cover all of it.
    • Let the baklava cool for at least 2-3 hours, or overnight, so that the syrup is absorbed and the flavors meld together.
  8. Serve:
    • Once cooled and soaked, the baklava is ready to be served!

Enjoy the crispy, nutty, and syrup-soaked Greek baklava, perfect as a sweet treat for any occasion!
