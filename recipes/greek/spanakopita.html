Spanakopita Recipe

A savory pie filled with spinach, feta and herbs.

Ingredients

For the Filling:
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 pounds (900g) fresh spinach (or 1 pound frozen spinach, thawed and drained)
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/2 cup (120g) ricotta cheese
  • 1/2 cup (120g) feta cheese, crumbled
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 egg, lightly beaten
For the Phyllo Layers:
  • 1 package phyllo dough (about 20 sheets), thawed if frozen
  • 1/2 cup (120g) unsalted butter, melted (or olive oil for a lighter version)

Preparation Time

  • Prep time: 30 minutes
  • Cook time: 40 minutes
  • Total time: 1 hour 10 minutes

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Filling:
    • In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic, and sauté for 5-7 minutes until softened.
    • Add the spinach (if using fresh, cook it down until wilted, and if using frozen, ensure it's thoroughly drained of excess water). Stir in salt, pepper, and nutmeg, and cook for another 2-3 minutes.
    • Remove from heat and let cool for 10 minutes.
    • Once cooled, stir in the ricotta, feta, Parmesan, and beaten egg. Mix until the filling is well combined.
  2. Prepare the Phyllo Dough:
    • Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
    • Brush a 9x13-inch (or similar sized) baking dish with some of the melted butter or olive oil.
    • Place a sheet of phyllo dough in the dish, and brush it with butter. Continue layering and buttering the phyllo sheets, using about 8-10 sheets for the bottom layer, ensuring each sheet is covered in butter.
  3. Assemble the Spanakopita:
    • Spread the spinach filling evenly over the layered phyllo sheets.
    • Continue layering phyllo sheets on top of the filling, brushing each sheet with butter as before. Use the remaining phyllo dough (about 8-10 sheets) to cover the top of the pie.
    • Tuck the edges of the phyllo dough into the sides of the pan to seal the filling.
  4. Bake:
    • Score the top of the spanakopita with a sharp knife, cutting it into squares or diamonds to make it easier to serve after baking.
    • Bake in the preheated oven for 35-40 minutes, or until the phyllo is golden and crispy.
  5. Serve:
    • Let the spanakopita cool for 10-15 minutes before cutting along the scored lines. Serve warm or at room temperature.

Enjoy this delicious and flaky Greek pastry filled with spinach and feta!
