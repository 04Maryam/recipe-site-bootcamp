Enchildas Recipe

Tortillas rolled around a filling and covered with chili sauce.

Ingredients

For the Enchiladas:
  • 12 corn tortillas
  • 2 cups cooked and shredded chicken (or beef, or cheese for vegetarian)
  • 1/2 cup onion, finely chopped
  • 1 1/2 cups enchilada sauce (store-bought or homemade)
  • 2 cups shredded cheese (cheddar, Monterrey Jack, or a Mexican blend)
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil (for frying the tortillas)
For the Homemade Enchilada Sauce (optional):
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 tablespoon flour
  • 2 tablespoons chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 1/2 cups chicken broth (or vegetable broth)
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon sugar (optional, to balance acidity)
For Garnish:
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped
  • Sour cream (optional)
  • Lime wedges (optional)

Preparation Time

  • Prep time: 15 minutes
  • Cook time: 25-30 minutes
  • Total time: ~40 minutes

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Enchilada Sauce (if homemade):
    • In a medium saucepan, heat 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the flour and stir for 1-2 minutes to form a roux.
    • Stir in the chili powder, cumin, garlic powder, onion powder, and salt. Cook for 1 minute until fragrant.
    • Gradually add the chicken broth, tomato paste, and sugar (if using), stirring to combine. Bring to a simmer and cook for 5-7 minutes until the sauce thickens.
    • Remove from heat and set aside.
  2. Prepare the Tortillas:
    • Heat the vegetable oil in a skillet over medium heat. Lightly fry the corn tortillas for 10-15 seconds on each side just to soften them (not crisp). Place them on a paper towel to absorb any excess oil.
  3. Assemble the Enchiladas:
    • Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
    • Spread a thin layer of enchilada sauce on the bottom of a baking dish.
    • Take each tortilla and dip it into the enchilada sauce, coating it completely.
    • Place the tortilla on a plate, and add a spoonful of shredded chicken, a little chopped onion, and a sprinkle of cheese in the center. Roll up the tortilla tightly and place it seam-side down in the baking dish.
    • Repeat this process until all tortillas are filled and placed in the dish.
  4. Top and Bake:
    • Pour the remaining enchilada sauce over the rolled tortillas, covering them evenly.
    • Sprinkle the remaining shredded cheese on top of the sauce.
    • Bake in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  5. Serve:
    • Remove the enchiladas from the oven and let them cool for a few minutes.
    • Garnish with chopped cilantro, sour cream, and lime wedges, if desired.

Enchiladas are a comforting and flavorful dish that's perfect for any gathering or family meal. The combination of soft, saucy tortillas, savory filling, and melted cheese makes these enchiladas a true crowd-pleaser. Whether you stick to traditional chicken, beef, or go vegetarian, this dish is sure to delight!
