Tacos al Pastor Recipe

Corn tortillas filled with marinated pork, pineapple, and onions.

Ingredients

For the Marinade:
  • 2 lb (900g) pork shoulder or pork loin, thinly sliced
  • 3 dried ancho chilies
  • 2 dried guajillo chilies
  • 1 tablespoon adobo sauce (from the chipotle can)
  • 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar
  • 1/2 cup pineapple juice (fresh is best)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
For the Tacos:
  • Corn tortillas (about 12)
  • 1 cup fresh pineapple, diced (for topping)
  • 1/2 cup white onion, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup cilantro, chopped
  • Lime wedges (for serving)
  • Salsa (optional, for serving)
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil (for cooking)

Preparation Time

  • Prep time: 20 minutes (plus 2-4 hours for marinating)
  • Cook time: 15-20 minutes
  • Total time: ~35-40 minutes (plus marination time)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Marinade:
    • Remove the stems and seeds from the dried ancho and guajillo chilies.
    • Toast the chilies in a dry skillet over medium heat for 1-2 minutes until fragrant, then soak them in hot water for about 10 minutes to soften.
    • Once softened, blend the chilies in a blender with the adobo sauce, apple cider vinegar, pineapple juice, garlic, cumin, oregano, cloves, paprika, brown sugar, black pepper, and salt until smooth.
  2. Marinate the Pork:
    • Slice the pork shoulder or loin into thin strips, about 1/4 inch thick.
    • In a large bowl, toss the pork with the marinade, ensuring it's well-coated. Cover and refrigerate for at least 2-4 hours, or overnight for the best flavor.
  3. Cook the Pork:
    • Heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil in a large skillet or grill pan over medium-high heat.
    • Add the marinated pork to the pan in batches (to avoid overcrowding) and cook for about 3-4 minutes per side, until the pork is nicely browned and cooked through.
  4. Prepare the Tacos:
    • While the pork is cooking, heat the corn tortillas in a dry skillet or on a grill for about 30 seconds on each side until warm and slightly charred.
    • Dice the fresh pineapple and chop the onion and cilantro for garnish.
  5. Assemble the Tacos:
    • Once the pork is cooked, chop it into smaller pieces (if needed) and place it on the warm tortillas.
    • Top with the diced pineapple, chopped onion, and cilantro.
  6. Serve:
    • Serve the tacos with lime wedges on the side for squeezing, and salsa if desired.
  7. Enjoy:
    • Enjoy the sweet, savory, and slightly smoky flavors of Tacos al Pastor!

Tacos al Pastor is a beloved Mexican dish that combines tender, marinated pork with the sweet kick of pineapple and a savory sauce. The dish is perfect for a taco night or casual gathering, bringing vibrant flavors and textures to your table.
