Green Curry Recipe

A spicy curry with coconut milk, green chili paste, and vegetables.

Ingredients

For the Curry Paste:
  • 4-6 green chilies (adjust to your spice preference)
  • 2 shallots, peeled and chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, peeled
  • 1 stalk lemongrass, chopped into pieces
  • 1-inch piece of galangal or ginger, peeled
  • 1/2 cup fresh cilantro (including stems)
  • 1 tablespoon ground cumin
  • 1 tablespoon ground coriander
  • Zest of 1 lime
  • 1 tablespoon shrimp paste (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce (or soy sauce for a vegetarian version)
For the Green Curry:
  • 1-2 tablespoons vegetable oil (or coconut oil)
  • 1 can (14 oz) coconut milk (full-fat for a richer taste)
  • 1-2 tablespoons green curry paste (adjust based on preferred spice level)
  • 1 lb (450g) chicken, beef, or tofu (cut into bite-sized pieces)
  • 1 medium eggplant, cut into cubes
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1/2 cup bamboo shoots (optional)
  • 1 cup Thai basil leaves
  • 1-2 tablespoons fish sauce (or soy sauce for vegetarian)
  • 1 tablespoon sugar (palm sugar preferred)
  • 1-2 tablespoons lime juice
  • 1 cup Thai jasmine rice (for serving)

Preparation Time

  • Prep time: 20-25 minutes
  • Cook time: 15-20 minutes
  • Total time: ~35-45 minutes

Instructions

  1. Make the Green Curry Paste:
    • Place the green chilies, shallots, garlic, lemongrass, galangal (or ginger), cilantro stems, ground cumin, ground coriander, lime zest, shrimp paste (if using), and fish sauce in a blender or mortar and pestle.
    • Blend or pound until a smooth, aromatic paste forms. Add a little water if needed to help blend.
  2. Cook the Green Curry:
    • Heat the vegetable oil (or coconut oil) in a large pot or wok over medium heat. Add the green curry paste and saut√© for 2-3 minutes, until fragrant and the oil begins to separate from the paste.
  3. Add the Coconut Milk:
    • Pour in the coconut milk and stir to combine with the curry paste. Bring the mixture to a simmer.
  4. Add the Protein and Vegetables:
    • Add the chicken, beef, or tofu to the curry and cook until the protein is nearly done (about 5-7 minutes for chicken).
    • Add the eggplant, red bell pepper, and bamboo shoots (if using). Simmer the curry for another 5-7 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender.
  5. Season the Curry:
    • Stir in the fish sauce (or soy sauce), sugar, and lime juice. Taste the curry and adjust the seasoning if needed. You can add more fish sauce for saltiness, lime juice for tartness, or sugar for sweetness.
  6. Finish the Curry:
    • Once the curry is cooked and flavorful, stir in the fresh Thai basil leaves. Let them wilt in the hot curry.
  7. Serve:
    • Serve the green curry hot over a bowl of steamed Thai jasmine rice.

Thai Green Curry is a flavorful, fragrant dish that brings together the richness of coconut milk with the heat of green chilies and the freshness of herbs. The green curry paste can be made from scratch or purchased, but making it fresh adds layers of depth to the dish. This curry is customizable with your choice of protein and vegetables, and it's perfect for a comforting dinner that packs a punch!
