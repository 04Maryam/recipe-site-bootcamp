Pad Thai Recipe

Stir-fried rice noodles with shrimp, peanuts, egg, and tamarind sauce.

Ingredients

For the Pad Thai:
  • 8 oz (225g) rice noodles (preferably flat, 1/4 inch wide)
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 eggs, lightly beaten
  • 1/2 cup firm tofu, cubed (or chicken, shrimp, or beef)
  • 1/2 cup bean sprouts
  • 1/4 cup chopped green onions (scallions)
  • 1/4 cup roasted peanuts, crushed
  • 1 lime, cut into wedges
  • 1 tablespoon cilantro, chopped (optional)
For the Pad Thai Sauce:
  • 3 tablespoons fish sauce (or soy sauce for vegetarian version)
  • 1 tablespoon sugar (preferably palm sugar)
  • 1 tablespoon tamarind paste
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon chili flakes (optional, for heat)
  • 1/2 cup water

Preparation Time

  • Prep time: 15 minutes
  • Cook time: 10-12 minutes
  • Total time: ~20-25 minutes

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Noodles:
    • Cook the rice noodles according to the package instructions (usually soaking in warm water for 5-10 minutes, then draining). Set aside.
  2. Make the Sauce:
    • In a small bowl, combine the fish sauce (or soy sauce), sugar, tamarind paste, rice vinegar, chili flakes (if using), and water. Stir well until the sugar dissolves. Set aside.
  3. Stir-Fry the Protein and Vegetables:
    • Heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat.
    • If using tofu, add it to the skillet and cook for 2-3 minutes on each side until lightly golden. If using chicken, shrimp, or beef, cook until fully cooked through, about 4-5 minutes. Remove the protein from the skillet and set it aside.
  4. Scramble the Eggs:
    • In the same skillet, add the remaining 1 tablespoon of oil. Pour the beaten eggs into the skillet and cook, stirring gently, until they are scrambled and cooked through. Remove and set aside with the protein.
  5. Combine and Stir-Fry:
    • In the same skillet, add the cooked rice noodles, and pour the prepared sauce over the noodles. Toss the noodles in the sauce, coating them evenly.
    • Add the cooked protein, scrambled eggs, bean sprouts, and half of the green onions. Stir-fry for another 1-2 minutes until everything is well combined and heated through.
  6. Serve:
    • Divide the Pad Thai onto serving plates.
    • Top with crushed peanuts, the remaining green onions, cilantro (if using), and a lime wedge on the side for squeezing.
    • Serve immediately while hot!

Pad Thai is a vibrant and flavorful Thai street food classic that combines savory, sweet, sour, and spicy elements into a satisfying dish. With its balance of fresh vegetables, protein, and crunchy peanuts, it's a perfect choice for a delicious and quick weeknight meal. Enjoy the flavors of Thailand right at home!
