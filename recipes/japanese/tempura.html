Tempura Recipe

Lightly battered and fried vegetables or seafood.

Ingredients

For the Tempura Batter:
  • 1 cup (120g) all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup (30g) cornstarch
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 large egg, beaten
  • 1 cup (240ml) ice-cold sparkling water or cold water
  • A pinch of salt
For the Tempura:
  • 12-16 shrimp, peeled and deveined, tails on
  • 1 medium sweet potato, thinly sliced
  • 1 small zucchini, thinly sliced
  • 1 small eggplant, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup (60g) mushrooms (shiitake or button), whole or halved
  • Vegetable oil (for deep frying)
For the Dipping Sauce:
  • 1/4 cup (60ml) soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup (60ml) mirin (sweet rice wine)
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon grated daikon radish (optional)

Preparation Time

  • Prep time: 20 minutes
  • Cook time: 10-15 minutes
  • Total time: ~35 minutes

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Dipping Sauce:
    • In a small bowl, combine the soy sauce, mirin, rice vinegar, and grated daikon (if using). Stir well and set aside.
  2. Prepare the Ingredients for Frying:
    • Slice the vegetables (sweet potato, zucchini, eggplant, and mushrooms) into thin pieces to ensure they cook evenly.
    • If using shrimp, ensure they are peeled, deveined, and the tails are left on.
  3. Make the Tempura Batter:
    • In a medium bowl, combine the flour, cornstarch, baking powder, and a pinch of salt.
    • Add the beaten egg and ice-cold sparkling water (or cold water) to the dry ingredients, mixing gently with chopsticks or a fork. The batter should be slightly lumpy (don’t overmix). Keep the batter cold by placing it in a bowl set over ice.
  4. Heat the Oil:
    • In a deep frying pan or wok, heat about 2 inches of vegetable oil over medium-high heat. The oil should reach 350°F (175°C) for the best frying temperature.
  5. Fry the Tempura:
    • Dip the shrimp and vegetables into the cold batter, allowing any excess to drip off, then carefully place them in the hot oil.
    • Fry in small batches, ensuring not to overcrowd the pan. Fry for about 2-3 minutes until golden and crispy.
    • Remove the tempura from the oil using a slotted spoon and drain on paper towels.
  6. Serve:
    • Serve the tempura immediately while hot and crispy, with the dipping sauce on the side.

Enjoy the crispy, delicate tempura, perfect as an appetizer or main course!
