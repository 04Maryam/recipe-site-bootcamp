Kunafa Recipe

A dessert made with shredded phyllo dough, cheese, and sweet syrup.

Ingredients

For the Kunafa:
  • 1 pound (450g) kataifi (shredded phyllo dough) or fine semolina (for traditional style)
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter (melted)
  • 2 cups ricotta cheese (or a combination of ricotta and mozzarella)
  • 1/2 cup sugar (for the filling)
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon (optional, for flavor)
  • 1/4 cup ground pistachios (for garnish, optional)
For the Sugar Syrup:
  • 1 1/2 cups sugar
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon rose water or orange blossom water (optional, for fragrance)

Preparation Time

  • Prep time: 20 minutes
  • Cook time: 30-35 minutes
  • Total time: ~50 minutes

Instructions

  1. Make the Sugar Syrup:
    • In a small saucepan, combine the sugar, water, and lemon juice. Bring to a boil over medium heat, stirring occasionally until the sugar has dissolved.
    • Reduce the heat and simmer for 10 minutes.
    • Remove from heat and stir in the rose water or orange blossom water if using. Set the syrup aside to cool.
  2. Prepare the Kataifi (Shredded Phyllo Dough):
    • If using kataifi dough, untangle and separate the strands gently. If using semolina, you can skip this step.
    • Pour the melted butter over the kataifi dough (or semolina) and toss to coat evenly. This will ensure the pastry is crispy and golden once baked.
  3. Assemble the Kunafa:
    • Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
    • Grease a 9-inch (23 cm) round or square baking dish with butter.
    • Layer half of the buttered kataifi dough in the bottom of the dish, pressing it down to form a firm base.
    • Mix the ricotta cheese with sugar and cinnamon (if using) and spread the cheese mixture evenly over the dough.
    • Top with the remaining buttered kataifi dough, spreading it evenly over the cheese filling.
  4. Bake the Kunafa:
    • Bake the assembled kunafa in the preheated oven for 30-35 minutes, or until the top is golden and crispy. The cheese should be melting inside, and the pastry will become beautifully golden brown.
  5. Serve:
    • Once out of the oven, immediately pour the cooled sugar syrup over the hot kunafa. Let it soak in for a few minutes.
    • Garnish with ground pistachios for added flavor and texture (optional).
    • Serve warm, sliced into squares or wedges.

Kunafa is a delightful Middle Eastern dessert with a perfect balance of crispy and gooey textures. A true crowd-pleaser for any celebration or special occasion!
