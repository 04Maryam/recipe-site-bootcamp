Tabboulah Recipe

A refreshing salad with bulgur, parsley, mint, and lemon juice.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup fine bulgur wheat
  • 2 cups boiling water
  • 2 cups fresh parsley, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup fresh mint leaves, finely chopped
  • 2 large tomatoes, diced
  • 1/2 cucumber, diced (optional, for extra crunch)
  • 1/4 cup red onion, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup fresh lemon juice (about 2 lemons)
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt to taste (about 1/2 teaspoon)
  • Freshly ground black pepper to taste (optional)

Preparation Time

  • Prep time: 15 minutes
  • Cook time: 10 minutes (for the bulgur to soften)
  • Total time: ~25 minutes

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Bulgur:
    • In a large bowl, pour 1 cup of fine bulgur wheat.
    • Pour the 2 cups of boiling water over the bulgur. Cover the bowl with a lid or plastic wrap and let it sit for about 10 minutes, until the bulgur softens and absorbs the water. Fluff the bulgur with a fork to separate the grains.
  2. Prepare the Vegetables and Herbs:
    • While the bulgur is soaking, finely chop the parsley, mint, tomatoes, cucumber (if using), and red onion.
  3. Combine the Ingredients:
    • Once the bulgur has softened, add the chopped parsley, mint, tomatoes, cucumber, and onion to the bulgur in the bowl.
    • Drizzle with the fresh lemon juice and olive oil. Toss everything together gently to combine.
  4. Season and Serve:
    • Season with salt to taste, and add freshly ground black pepper if desired.
    • Stir everything together again and taste to adjust the seasoning, adding more lemon juice, olive oil, or salt as needed.
  5. Serve:
    • Tabbouleh is best served chilled or at room temperature. It makes a refreshing side dish or light salad for a summer meal or Mediterranean platter.

Tabbouleh is a refreshing, herb-packed salad that captures the essence of Mediterranean flavors, perfect for warm weather or a light meal.
