Kung Pao Chicken Recipe

Ingredients

For the Chicken Marinade:
  • 500g (1 lb) boneless, skinless chicken breast, cut into small cubes
  • 2 teaspoons soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon cornstarch
  • 1 teaspoon rice wine (optional)
For the Sauce:
  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon hoisin sauce
  • 1 tablespoon dark soy sauce (optional, for deeper color)
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar or white vinegar
  • 2 teaspoons sugar
  • 2 teaspoons cornstarch
  • 4 tablespoons water
For the Stir-Fry:
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • 6-8 dried red chilies (adjust to your spice preference)
  • 1 teaspoon Sichuan peppercorns (optional, for authentic flavor)
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, minced
  • 1/2 cup roasted peanuts
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced
  • 3 green onions, chopped into 1-inch pieces

Preparation Time

  • Prep time: 15 minutes
  • Cook time: 10 minutes
  • Total time: 25 minutes

Instructions

  1. Marinate the Chicken: In a bowl, mix the chicken with soy sauce, cornstarch, and rice wine (if using). Let it marinate for 10-15 minutes while you prepare the other ingredients.
  2. Prepare the Sauce: In a small bowl, combine soy sauce, hoisin sauce, dark soy sauce (if using), rice vinegar, sugar, cornstarch, and water. Stir well until the cornstarch dissolves. Set aside.
  3. Stir-Fry the Chicken: Heat 1 tablespoon of oil in a wok or large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the marinated chicken and stir-fry until lightly browned and cooked through (about 3-4 minutes). Remove the chicken and set it aside.
  4. Cook the Aromatics and Vegetables: Add the remaining 1 tablespoon of oil to the wok. Toss in the dried red chilies and Sichuan peppercorns (if using) and stir-fry for 30 seconds until fragrant. Add the garlic and ginger, stirring for another 30 seconds.
  5. Combine Ingredients: Add the bell peppers and green onions to the wok. Stir-fry for 1-2 minutes until slightly tender but still crisp. Return the chicken to the wok and pour in the prepared sauce. Stir everything together, cooking for 1-2 minutes until the sauce thickens and evenly coats the ingredients.
  6. Finish with Peanuts: Stir in the roasted peanuts and give everything one final mix.
  7. Serve: Serve hot with steamed rice for a complete meal.
