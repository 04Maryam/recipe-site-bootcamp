Dim Sum Recipe

Steamed dumplings filled with shrimp, chicken or vegetables.

Ingredients

For the Dough:
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 3/4 cup boiling water
  • 1/4 cup cold water
For the Filling:
  • 300g (10 oz) chicken
  • 1/2 cup shrimp, finely chopped (optional)
  • 1/4 cup water chestnuts, finely chopped
  • 2 green onions, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 1 teaspoon cornstarch
  • Pinch of salt and pepper
For Serving:
  • Soy sauce
  • Chili oil
  • Black vinegar

Preparation Time

  • Prep time: 30 minutes
  • Cook time: 10-12 minutes per batch
  • Total time: ~1 hour

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Dough:
    • In a mixing bowl, combine the flour and boiling water. Mix with chopsticks or a spoon until it forms a rough dough.
    • Gradually add the cold water, kneading the dough until it becomes smooth and elastic. Cover with a damp cloth and let it rest for 30 minutes.
  2. Prepare the Filling:
    • In a bowl, combine ground pork or chicken, shrimp (if using), water chestnuts, green onions, soy sauce, oyster sauce, sesame oil, ginger, cornstarch, salt, and pepper. Mix until well combined. Cover and refrigerate until ready to use.
  3. Shape the Dumplings:
    • Roll the dough into a long log and cut it into 20-24 equal pieces. Roll each piece into a small ball.
    • Flatten each ball with a rolling pin to form a thin, round wrapper (about 3 inches in diameter).
    • Place a teaspoon of filling in the center of each wrapper. Fold the wrapper in half and pleat the edges to seal the dumpling tightly.
  4. Steam the Dumplings:
    • Line a bamboo or metal steamer with parchment paper or cabbage leaves to prevent sticking.
    • Arrange the dumplings in the steamer, leaving space between them to expand.
    • Bring a pot of water to a boil and place the steamer over it. Cover and steam the dumplings for 10-12 minutes until the filling is fully cooked.
  5. Serve:
    • Serve the dumplings hot with dipping sauces like soy sauce, chili oil, or black vinegar.

Enjoy your freshly steamed dim sum!
