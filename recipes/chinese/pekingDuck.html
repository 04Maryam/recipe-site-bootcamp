Peking Duck Recipe

Roasted duck with crispy skin, served with pancakes and hoisin sauce.

Ingredients

For the Duck:
  • 1 whole duck (about 2-2.5 kg or 4.5-5.5 lbs)
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • 2 cups boiling water
  • 1 teaspoon five-spice powder
For Serving:
  • Mandarin pancakes (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1 cucumber, julienned
  • 4 green onions, sliced into thin strips
  • Hoisin sauce

Preparation Time

  • Prep time: 30 minutes
  • Resting time: 4-6 hours (or overnight)
  • Cook time: 1.5-2 hours
  • Total time: ~6-8 hours

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Duck:
    • Rinse the duck under cold water and pat it dry with paper towels. Trim any excess fat and remove the giblets if included.
    • Use a sharp skewer or fork to prick small holes all over the skin. This helps the fat render and the skin crisp during roasting.
  2. Make the Glaze:
    • In a small bowl, mix the honey, soy sauce, and rice vinegar. Set aside.
  3. Blanch the Duck:
    • Bring 2 cups of water to a boil. Slowly pour the boiling water over the duck to tighten the skin. Let the duck cool and dry completely.
  4. Season the Duck:
    • Rub the duck inside and out with the five-spice powder. Brush the glaze over the entire duck.
  5. Dry the Duck:
    • Hang the duck in a cool, well-ventilated place (or place it on a rack in the fridge) uncovered for 4-6 hours or overnight. This step ensures a crispier skin.
  6. Roast the Duck:
    • Preheat the oven to 200째C (400째F). Place the duck breast-side up on a rack in a roasting pan.
    • Roast for 30 minutes, then lower the temperature to 180째C (350째F).
    • Continue roasting for another 60-90 minutes, brushing the duck with the glaze every 20 minutes, until the skin is golden and crisp.
  7. Prepare the Pancakes and Garnishes:
    • Warm the Mandarin pancakes as per package instructions or prepare homemade ones.
    • Arrange julienned cucumber, sliced green onions, and hoisin sauce on a serving platter.
  8. Carve the Duck:
    • Let the duck rest for 10 minutes after roasting. Use a sharp knife to carve thin slices of the crispy skin and meat.
  9. Serve:
    • Place a slice of duck with some skin, cucumber, and green onion on a pancake. Add hoisin sauce, roll it up, and enjoy!

Enjoy your homemade Peking Duck with its signature crispy skin and rich flavors!
